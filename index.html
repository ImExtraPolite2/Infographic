Due to their immense gravitational pull, black holes cause time dilation, meaning time moves slower for an observer near a black hole compared to someone farther away.

THEY EMIT RADIATION

Although black holes are known for trapping everything, they can slowly evaporate over time due to Hawking radiation, a quantum process where particle-antiparticle pairs form near the event horizon.

THEY CAN MERGE

When two black holes collide, they create massive gravitational waves, ripples in spacetime that can be detected by observatories like LIGO and Virgo.

THEY CAN GROW

By consuming gas, dust, stars, or even merging with other black holes, they can increase in size and mass.

THEY FORM FROM COLLAPSED STARS

Most black holes originate when massive stars exhaust their fuel and collapse under their own gravity.
